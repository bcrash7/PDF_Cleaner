## Current State Of The Industry

Today's healthcare industry is facing unprecedented challenges. Staffing shortages, high costs and policy changes are plaguing our hospitals, health systems and local medical offices. As a result of these challenges, patients across the country are feeling the strain-long wait times, high costs and disconnected provider relationships have become the norm.

Patients are growing tired of traditional, sometimes outdated, cookie-cutter care that fails to meet their personal health needs, schedules and budgets. This is leading many to leverage telehealth, seek online solutions and find more personalized approaches to healthcare delivery.

## Growing Demand For Personalized Medicine

Each individual has unique needs and characteristics physiologically, molecularly, genetically, environmentally and behaviorally that impact their health. However, traditional healthcare delivery generally fails to acknowledge and address this. A growing number of patients are seeking self-service solutions online, which can cut direct interaction with a medical provider out of the picture.

As such, there is an immediate need for investment in methods of care that both keep the patient's unique needs and lifestyle in mind but also maintain safe medical oversight. Collaborative healthcare delivery solutions that target the right treatment, for the right patient, at the right time, could shape medical paradigms of the future. And since it's been proven that quality care delivery and patient engagement have a direct correlation with health outcomes, a large opportunity for health and business leaders is present.

New concepts in bioinformatics, immunotherapy and pharmacogenetics, to name a few, are enabling customized, convenient treatments. One such example is in my area of expertise-allergy treatment.

Other examples include cancer patients having the option to receive chemotherapy from the comfort of home. And sleep apnea patients can choose an implant or oral appliance instead of the traditionally prescribed continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) therapy. My own company is already part of this trend, as well.

## Concierge Medicine: An Indicator Of Change

A notable trend that indicates patients are ready for significant change in how they receive healthcare is concierge medicine. Sometimes referred to as boutique medicine, retainer-based medicine or direct primary careconcierge medicine is membership-based healthcare that pairs exclusive, personalized care with accessibility and convenience.

This form of privatization of services has become a movement in pockets of the U.S. in the past several years. This is reflected in the growth of the concierge medicine market, which was estimated at $6.7 billion last year, and projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate of 10.37% from 2024 to 2030. As business leaders, we can view this increase in concierge medicine as a strong indicator that patients are looking for a major shift from the status quo of healthcare delivery.

## In Conclusion

As patients continue to demand change, companies would be wise to consider investing in models of care that put patients at the center of their own care teams. There is a growing number of companies and digital health technologies that personalize and improve patient care, and we can expect this trend to continue in 2024 and beyond.
