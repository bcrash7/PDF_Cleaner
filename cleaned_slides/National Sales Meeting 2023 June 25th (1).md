Why we LOSE deals: (Presuming you mean to a direct competitor)

 

1. Mediocrity across key channels of salesmanship:
2. Lack of consistent, timely follow up

MEDDIC-F

Welcome to this training session on MEDDIC, a powerful sales qualification framework. MEDDIC provides a systematic approach to understanding customer needs, aligning sales efforts, and increasing the chances of closing deals successfully. In this training, we'll explore each element of MEDDIC and discuss how to effectively apply it in your sales process.

MEDDIC-F (Metrics, Economic buyer, Decision criteria, Decision process, Identify pain, Champion, and Fox)

<!-- image -->

MEDDIC-F

Metrics:

The first element of MEDDIC is Metrics. This refers to the quantifiable measures of success or goals that the customer wants to achieve. In this step, it's crucial to identify the key performance indicators (KPIs) or metrics that are important to your customer. Understand their business objectives and how your solution can help them achieve those objectives. By aligning your offering with their metrics, you can demonstrate the value you bring to the table.

Key points to cover:

How to identify the customer's metrics and KPIs.

How to relate your solution to the customer's metrics.

The importance of quantifying the value of your offering.

<!-- image -->

MEDDIC-F

Economic Buyer:

The second element of MEDDIC is the Economic Buyer. This refers to the person or group of people who have the authority to make the purchasing decision and control the budget. It's crucial to identify and engage with the economic buyer early in the sales process. Understanding their needs, priorities, and decision-making criteria is essential for successful selling.

Key points to cover:

How to identify the economic buyer.

Strategies for engaging and building relationships with economic buyers.

Understanding the economic buyer's priorities and decision-making process.

<!-- image -->

MEDDIC-F

Decision Criteria:

The third element of MEDDIC is Decision Criteria. This involves understanding the specific factors or criteria the customer will consider when evaluating your solution. By understanding their decision-making process, you can tailor your approach and address their concerns effectively. It's important to proactively gather information about their decision criteria and align your offering accordingly.

Key points to cover:

How to identify and prioritize the customer's decision criteria.

Techniques for gathering information about decision criteria.

Tailoring your solution to meet the customer's decision criteria.

<!-- image -->

MEDDIC-F

Decision Process:

The fourth element of MEDDIC is the Decision Process. This refers to the steps and stakeholders involved in the customer's decision-making process. Understanding the decision process allows you to navigate through the organization, build relationships with key stakeholders, and influence the decision in your favor. By identifying the decision-makers, influencers, and gatekeepers, you can effectively manage the sales cycle.

Key points to cover:

Mapping the decision-making process and identifying stakeholders.

Building relationships with key stakeholders.

Strategies for navigating complex decision processes.

<!-- image -->

MEDDIC-F

Identify Pain:

The fifth element of MEDDIC is to Identify Pain. This involves understanding the customer's pain points, challenges, or problems that your solution can address. By demonstrating a clear understanding of their pain points, you position yourself as a trusted advisor who can provide valuable solutions. Probing questions and active listening are crucial in uncovering and understanding the customer's pain.

Key points to cover:

Techniques for uncovering customer pain points.

Active listening and effective questioning strategies.

Linking your solution to the customer's pain points.

MEDDIC-F

Champion:

The final element of MEDDIC is the Champion. This refers to a key individual within the customer organization who supports and advocates for your solution. Having a champion can significantly increase your chances of success. It's important to identify and nurture champions throughout the sales process, as they can help you navigate the organization, address concerns, and influence the decision.

Key points to cover:

How to identify and build relationships with champions.

Leveraging champions to influence the decision.

The role of champions in driving successful sales.

<!-- image -->

MEDDIC-F

FOX:

The additional element of MEDDIC is the FOX. This refers to a key individual within the practice or their close friends and family who will try to tank your deal. There is always someone within the practice can significantly decrease your chances of success. It's important to identify the FOX throughout the sales process, as will want to address concerns, and influence the decision.

Key points to cover:

How to identify and build relationships with the FOX

Leveraging champions to influence the FOX.

The role of FOX in killing your deal
